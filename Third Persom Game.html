Third Person Game

A short linear story-driven action game, where the play has a sword with wich he can perform 2 attacks; light and heavy , can roll, switch to a gun and shoot.

The enemies in the game have simple AI:

  • The melee enemies follow you if detected and attack if they reach you.
  • Some of the melee enemies can patrol between points.
  • The range enemies stay still but fire at you if you enter their field of view.
  • The game has one final boss with 2 phases, 1 rage attack and 4 different attacks (1 melee and 1 range per phase).

    Examples

  • Slopes:

    • Player's perspective: The player moves slowly going uphill and fast going downhills.

    Technical perspective: I calculate the DOT Product of the player's movement's direction and the ground's normal vector. The result acts as an offset percentage of the player's velocity. The offset percentage gets added to the velocity if going downhill and deducted if going uphill.

  • Stairs:

    • Player's perspective: The player does not have to jump to overcome a step. He just keeps moving.

    Technical perspective: Climbing a step is done in the following order: Cast in the movement's direction a ray from the player's feet -> If an object is detected -> Cast in the movement's direction a ray from the player's feet + height (Step max height) with a small distance -> If no object is detected -> Cast downward a ray from the player's feet + (height + small distance forward) -> Get a hitpoint -> Interpolate the player to that hitpoint using Coroutine.
